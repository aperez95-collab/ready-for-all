Ready for All

Advocating for Inclusive Disaster Preparedness – Support HB 1144

Why This Matters

During disasters, people with disabilities are too often left without support. Texas House Bill 1144 aims to change that by making disaster planning equitable, inclusive, and accessible for all.

Why It Matters: People with disabilities are 2 to 4 times more likely to be injured or die during a disaster than the general population. For instance, during the 2017 California wildfires, many individuals with disabilities faced life-threatening barriers to evacuation and shelter access. A 2022 National Council on Disability report found that emergency preparedness remains insufficient across states, with 60% of surveyed individuals reporting unmet needs during recent emergencies.

What HB 1144 Does

  • Requires inclusive emergency planning for people with disabilities
  • Ensures shelters are accessible and equipped
  • Improves emergency alerts and communication formats
  • Protects services that vulnerable communities rely on
